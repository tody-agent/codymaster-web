Chapter 1

The Problem I Couldn't Ignore

In late 2024, AI coding agents started becoming genuinely useful. I was a product manager with a decade of experience — I knew exactly what to build. But I had no idea how to build it.

So I did what every non-technical person does: I opened Claude, described what I wanted, and watched it generate code.

It was magical. For about three days.

Then reality hit. The AI designed my landing page differently every session. It fixed one bug and broke five others. It forgot my brand, my architecture, my decisions — every single morning I had to re-explain everything from scratch. I shipped something, and a secret key ended up in my GitHub history. I deployed to the wrong account twice.

"AI gave me 100 hands. Without discipline, those hands created chaos."
Chapter 2

The Obsession

I started researching how senior developers work. Not what they know — but how they think. I read Andrej Karpathy's notes on AI coding discipline. Robert C. Martin's Clean Code. I studied TRIZ, the Soviet-era innovation methodology used by engineers to solve impossible problems.

I found patterns. Senior devs don't guess — they trace root causes. They don't code first — they plan first. They don't deploy and pray — they test, then stage, then ship.

So I started writing these patterns as instructions for my AI agent. One by one. Each one solving a specific failure I'd experienced.

After 3 months, I had 30 skills. After 6 months, I had 50+.

Chapter 4
